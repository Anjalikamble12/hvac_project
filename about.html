Incorrectly Sized Air Conditioners

It is common for builders to install over-sized air conditioners because these units provide cooling more quickly, thus avoiding any chance of not meeting the cooling demand. Typically, A/C systems are sized based on the number of square feet in the house, usually about 1 ton of cooling for every 300-500 square feet, often with an extra ton or two thrown in for good measure. Another issue is when replacing a system is contractors who simply install a new system based on the old system's size..
Unfortunately, over-sized air conditioners “short-cycle” or run for shorter periods of time than engineered for optimum performance. The efficiency of air conditioners is poor when they first start and increases gradually, reaching peak efficiency in about 10 minutes. The problem with this method is that every house is unique. A house with low E windows, natural shading, a low rate of infiltration, and better insulation may need only 50% as much tonnage to cool as one without those features. More importantly, an over-sized A/C system will not be as effective at removing humidity from the air, resulting in a less comfortable home.

The Correct Way To Size An HVAC System

House Pro uses Air Conditioning Contractors of America (ACCA) Manual J Load Calculation. Our highly trained technicians will carefully measure your home and evaluate its levels of insulation. Windows’ sizes and quantity will be noted, levels of infiltration will be estimated and internal loads including lighting and kitchen appliances will be taken into consideration. A computerized analysis will be completed that indicates just how much heating and/or cooling capacity your new system should provide. After the installation of your new system is completed, you will receive a copy of the computerized load calculation for your records.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is HVAC maintenance important?
If you want to ensure the lifespan of your Houston HVAC systems, you need to perform regular inspections. The upkeep of your air conditioning and heater system will help you reduce the risk for HVAC repair and replacement. If you keep to its preventive maintenance schedule, the system will run at peak energy efficiency for a long time, so you won’t have to spend money on a brand new AC system or heater. You could end up saving 30% on your total energy bills by having your existing unit working at peak efficiency when you schedule HVAC service frequently in Houston.
How much does HVAC maintenance cost?
HVAC maintenance costs are considerably less than an AC repair or heating system installation or a replacement. If you need to have your cooling and heating systems repaired, you can expect to spend about $800. However, if it’s just a minor issue with the system, you can pay between $300 and $650, depending upon the HVAC repair services you need.
How often does HVAC system need to be maintained?
You should pay to get your HVAC system serviced twice a year if you want to avoid spending on unnecessary repairs that could have been prevented. If the air conditioning system is used all year round, maintaining it with regular services every six months is recommended.
What does HVAC maintenance include?
Our HVAC technicians' visits include inspecting the circuits, drain line, safety controls, caps, valves heat pumps, and condensation pumps. All system components are carefully checked to detect any signs of wear and tear or other possible issues. If the technician detects a problem, they will get it fixed before it has a chance to grow further. The technician will also test the entire system for its performance and any issues that don’t allow it to run as it should. Signing up for regular visits to your home or business will reduce AC repair costs. If you want your HVAC Houston to run efficiently all year round, never put off your inspections.
